About Prairie Pyro's Artist

Hi there!

My name is Stephanie and thank you for taking an interest in me and what I do! I've setup this information to be kind of like an interview/FAQ. If there's something here that I didn't cover, please contact me and I will update this section as well as answer your question(s)!

helpWhat is Pyrography/Woodburning?

Pyrography or pyrogravure is the art of decorating wood or other materials with burn marks resulting from the controlled application of a heated object such as a poker, or in my usecase, a woodburning machine made by Razertip. It is also known as pokerwork or wood burning. You can read more on wikipedia.

helpWhat got you into Pyrography/Woodburning?

I first learned about this art when I was in grade 9, around the age of 14 or 15. I was able to take a special class of my choosing which would happen every 10 business days and would last for the whole day. I chose Woodworking from the options given (there was Carpentry, Make Up and Hair, Sewing, etc). In this class, I learned how to woodburn, carve wood, use a scrollsaw and create art pieces called Instarsias. I took this class for two years in a row and had a knack for it right from the get go - Art talents do run in the family in various mediums. My parents bought me a machine when I was in grade 12 and I've been woodburning ever since.

helpWhen did you decide to start selling your art?

I started selling my art around 8 years ago (2011). A close fiend and my boyfriend at the time convinced me that I should start selling the art I'm making as well as take custom orders to do more. I was hesitant at first since I didn't know how much extra work it was going to be but I took the leap anyway and I haven't regretted it since. Kudos to them for giving me the push I needed to do this otherwise Prairie Pyro Designs wouldn't have been born. I was also starting to have quite a few finished pieces that were piling up that I wasn't quite sure what to do with so this also helped with the motivation. Once the first piece sold, it was super exciting and pushed me to start kicking things into high gear.

helpHow did you come up with Prairie Pyro Designs for a name?

I have two good friends to thank for this actually! Naming things is hard, as many know, so I saught out feedback from people in my friend circle. I knew I wanted Pyro in the name since it related to Pyrography. One friend suggested Prairie Pyro since I live in Saskatchewan, a prairie province in Canada. I loved it! Another friend suggested that I add Designs to the end so it sounded more professional. And just like that, Prairie Pyro Designs was born. I even have a mascot! It's the flame in my logo that you can see at the top of every page.

helpDo you do custom orders?

Yes! To request one, you can do so via my Etsy store or my Facebook page.

Note: I am unable to do custom work on a finished piece of wood - this includes if it has been varnished or stained. I work with unfinished wood only. I do not have the tools to remove the finish and then re-apply it. I am also unable to do portraits of people or work with photos. I will find linearts or sketches to work with so this could limit what I am able to do for you.
